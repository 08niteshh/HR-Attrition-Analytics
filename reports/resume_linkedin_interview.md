## Interview Answers

### Tell me about your HR attrition project.

I built an end-to-end HR attrition analytics project using Python, SQL, and Power BI. I cleaned 1,470 IBM employee records, removed constant-value fields, encoded categorical variables, and engineered salary, tenure, overtime, and attrition features. I then analyzed attrition across roles, pay bands, overtime, tenure, promotion gaps, and satisfaction scores. Finally, I created PostgreSQL queries and a dashboard plan for decision-makers.

### What was the most interesting insight?

The clearest insight was the impact of overtime. Employees working overtime had a 30.53% attrition rate, compared with 10.44% among employees without overtime. That is a 2.92x risk ratio. It suggests that workload is not merely an operational metric; it is an important retention signal.

### How would this analysis help an HR team?

The project turns broad attrition reporting into targeted action. HR can focus on specific high-risk roles, review workloads for overtime-heavy teams, prioritize compensation discussions for low-salary groups, and intervene early for new employees. The composite rule isolates 21 high-risk employees with a 71.43% historical attrition rate, making outreach more focused.
